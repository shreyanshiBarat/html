Coding, often referred to as computer programming, is the fundamental language used to communicate with computers and instruct them to perform specific tasks. It acts as a translator, converting human-written instructions into binary code—sequences of 0s and 1s—that machines understand. Through programming languages like Python, Java, and JavaScript, developers create instructions that function similar to a manual or recipe, guiding software on how to act. Coding is essential for developing modern technology, including websites, mobile apps, video games, and smart devices. Beyond just technical skill, learning to code fosters critical thinking, problem-solving, and creativity, acting as a crucial literacy in the modern digital age.
